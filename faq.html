Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know

What is the idea behind this research project?

This is an ongoing scientific study investigating how different types of cognitive training affect early mathematics learning in young children. The goal is to better understand how children develop mathematical skills and how digital training tools can support that development at scale.

What devices does Vektor run on?

Vektor runs on standard tablets. Sessions take place during regular school hours and no special hardware or installation is required beyond a tablet already available at the school.

How long is the training program?

The program runs for 8 weeks during regular school hours, with short daily sessions. Progress is assessed at a few points during the program. The total time commitment is designed to fit comfortably within the normal school day.

Has Vektor been tested before?

Yes. Vektor has been used in large-scale studies involving tens of thousands of children across multiple countries, with results published in leading scientific journals. The current project builds directly on this established evidence base.

Where is personal data stored?

All data is fully anonymised, stored on European servers, and handled in compliance with GDPR. Students are identified only by anonymous IDs — no names or personal details are stored in the app. Teachers and schools can request access to or deletion of their data at any time by contacting the research team.

What does the training look like for students?

Students work through a series of engaging, game-like tasks on a tablet. The app automatically adjusts difficulty based on each child's responses, so every child works at an appropriate level. Tasks are varied and designed to be fun and accessible for young children.

What is the teacher's role?

Teachers supervise sessions and can monitor overall class progress. The platform is designed to be straightforward to run — Vektor handles the training automatically. Teachers do not need to prepare lesson content. The research team is available by email for any practical questions.

Is this only for Grade 1?

Yes — in this research project we are currently recruiting Grade 1 classes (förskoleklass, age 5–6) in Swedish schools only. The focus on this age group is based on scientific reasons related to the timing of early mathematics development.

How many schools are involved?

The project aims to recruit approximately 10,000 Grade 1 pupils across Sweden. It is a large-scale study designed to produce statistically robust results representative of Swedish schools broadly.

Is Vektor free?

Yes, completely free. Participating schools and teachers pay nothing. The project is funded as part of an academic research programme at Karolinska Institutet. All materials, the app, and teacher support are provided at no cost.
